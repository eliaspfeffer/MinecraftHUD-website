// What it does

Eight features.
All Minecraft.

Every element of the HUD mirrors something real about your working day.

❤️

Health Bar = Battery

9 pixel-perfect Minecraft hearts mirror your battery percentage in real time. Drop below 20% and you'll hear the classic hurt sound. You've been warned.

🍗

Hunger Bar = Real Hunger

Takes 6 hours to empty — just like actual hunger during a workday. Three quick clicks on the drumsticks to eat and refill. Complete with the eating sound effect.

✨

XP Bar = Your Work

Every keystroke and mouse click earns XP. Watch the green bar fill. Level up sounds fire automatically. Resets at midnight. What's your daily high score?

💀

Kill Feed

Close any app and it appears top-right: "Chrome was slain." "Zoom has fallen." "Spotify got rekt." The kill feed fades out after a few seconds.

🎵

Real Minecraft Sounds

Original game sounds — directly from Mojang's CDN. Dirt on typing. Gravel on Delete. TNT on Force Quit. XP pickup on Save. Villager hmm on new tab.

🖥️

Fully Click-Through

The overlay sits above every app but gets out of your way completely. You won't even notice it — until someone walks by and asks what that thing on your screen is.

🐄

Walking Mobs

Press Cmd+C and a pixel art Minecraft cow walks across the bottom of your screen. Press Cmd+V and a pig trots by. Click them to interact. Cmd+Z barks.

🌤

Animated Background

Toggle a live Minecraft landscape behind all your windows. Clouds drift, birds fly, the sun arcs across the sky based on your real time of day. Stars and moon at night.

// Installation

Up in 60 seconds.

No installer. No setup wizard. No subscription. Just download and open.

01

Download & Install

Open the .dmg — drag MinecraftHUD to your Applications folder. macOS may say it's "damaged": go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → scroll down → click "Open Anyway".

02

Grant Accessibility

System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility → add MinecraftHUD. This lets it listen for keyboard events to play sounds and track XP.

03

You're In Survival Mode

The HUD appears at the bottom of your screen. Start typing. Close some apps. Hear the sounds. Try not to die.
